# Workflow Automation

## Page role
Explain workflow automation as practical operational relief, not tech-for-tech's-sake.

- **Priority:** 2
- **Primary audience:** small teams with too much manual follow-up or repetitive coordination work
- **Primary CTA:** `/contact?intent=service`
- **Primary proof:** `/work/the-launchpad`
- **Supporting proof:** `/work/urgentcare-indy`, `/work/primarycare-indy`

## H1 direction
Workflow automation that removes bottlenecks, repetitive work, and dropped follow-up.

## Plain-English definition
This service designs the processes and automations that make reminders, routing, handoffs, and repetitive actions happen more reliably.

## Why it matters
Manual work slows response times, creates inconsistency, and wastes team energy.

## Signs you need it
- follow-up depends on someone remembering
- internal handoffs break often
- staff repeats the same admin tasks daily
- reminders, routing, or task assignment are inconsistent

## What it usually includes
- process mapping
- trigger/action design
- reminder and routing logic
- task/status workflow cleanup
- operational handoff support

## Technical scope
Event-driven workflow logic, CRM/status triggers, notifications, routing rules, and cross-tool process automation.

## Proof block
Use The Launchpad as the primary systems proof. Use UrgentCare Indy and PrimaryCare Indy as supporting examples of operational workflow improvement.

## FAQ targets
- what should be automated first?
- can this work with our current tools?
- how custom does this get?
- how do you avoid over-automation?
