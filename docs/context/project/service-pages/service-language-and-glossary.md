# Service Language + Glossary Guide

Use this file to keep service pages readable for mixed-literacy buyers without creating separate beginner/advanced versions.

## Core rule

Do not ask visitors to choose a knowledge level.
Write pages in layers instead.

## Writing model

### Layer 1 — buyer language
Use this first.

Examples:
- your website is not pulling its weight
- leads are slipping through the cracks
- the follow-up is manual
- marketing and operations are not connected
- reporting exists, but no one trusts it

### Layer 2 — translator language
Use this next.

Examples:
- this work connects strategy, website structure, CRM, automation, and reporting
- the goal is to make growth easier to run and easier to measure
- this usually means fixing the gaps between the offer, the site, the systems, and the follow-up

### Layer 3 — specialist language
Use this lower on the page.

Examples:
- CRM architecture
- workflow automation
- lifecycle stages
- attribution cleanup
- internal-linking structure
- schema direction
- dashboard architecture

## Terms to define carefully

| Term | Plain-English translation |
|---|---|
| CRM architecture | the structure behind how leads move, who owns them, and how the system stays usable |
| Workflow automation | the automations that remove repetitive manual work and make follow-up more reliable |
| Conversion optimization | improving the parts of the site or funnel that turn visits into action |
| Lead generation system | the full path from attention to capture to follow-up |
| GEO / AI search readiness | making the site easier for AI-assisted systems to understand and surface |
| Reporting and decision systems | the smaller set of numbers and dashboards that help the business decide what to do next |

## Terms to avoid leading with

Do not start pages with these unless they are immediately translated:
- RevOps
- omnichannel
- martech stack
- entity optimization
- demand capture architecture
- orchestration layer

## Better replacements

| Avoid | Prefer |
|---|---|
| revops alignment | cleaner sales and marketing handoff |
| lifecycle automation | automated follow-up and stage movement |
| funnel optimization | better paths from visit to inquiry |
| martech stack | the tools and systems behind your marketing |
| GTM orchestration | launch and rollout structure |

## Page-writing checks

Before finalizing a page, ask:
1. Could a founder understand the opener without looking anything up?
2. Does the middle explain how the work connects to outcomes?
3. Does the technical section satisfy a more advanced buyer without taking over the page?
4. Is the CTA clear and singular?
5. Is the proof tied to the claim being made?

## Use case

Use this file alongside all service-page briefs to keep the voice:
- clear
