# Homepage + Services Content Map

This file explains how the homepage and the services section should work together now that service-page briefs exist.

It is the bridge between:
- homepage strategy
- services index structure
- sub-service page implementation
- proof and CTA routing

## Core rule

The homepage should orient.
The services index should explain.
The sub-service pages should clarify and convert.
The work pages should prove.
The tools should give immediate value.
The contact page should close the loop.

## Homepage role by section

| Homepage Section | Job | What it should communicate | Main next step |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hero | Identify the buyer + problem + promise | who this is for, what is broken, what Darling MarTech fixes, why the model is different | `/work` or `/contact` |
| Services intro / cards | Frame the problem clusters | strategy, websites, systems, growth — explained in buyer language first | `/services` |
| Featured tool | Offer immediate value | visitors can self-diagnose or clarify their next step | `/tools` |
| Selected proof | Show tight evidence | 4–5 highly relevant proof examples | `/work` |
| Testimonials | External validation | trust, working style, and credibility from real clients | `/contact` or `/work` |
| Contact CTA | Final next step | simple invitation into the right conversation | `/contact` |

## Homepage messaging rules

### The homepage should not:
- explain every sub-service in detail
- repeat the full founder story multiple times
- overwhelm cold visitors with internal service language
- ask users to choose a knowledge level

### The homepage should:
- lead with business problems, not service labels
- make the offer model legible quickly
- show where proof lives
- create clear next-step options for different visitor states

## Services index role

The services index should sit between the homepage and the sub-service pages.

### Job of the services index
- explain the four service clusters
- define them in plain English
- help visitors recognize which cluster matches their problem
- route them into the right child page
- support each cluster with proof and/or a useful adjacent tool

## Recommended services index cluster structure

### 1. Strategy & Leadership
Primary problems:
- no senior owner of the system
- unclear priorities
- weak positioning
- no coherent growth direction

Best child pages:
- Fractional CMO / Strategic Leadership
- Positioning & Messaging Strategy
- Marketing Audit & Growth Roadmap
- Go-to-Market / Launch Strategy

### 2. Websites, UX & Brand
Primary problems:
- the site underperforms
- trust is weak
- conversion flow is weak
- brand is unclear or inconsistent

Best child pages:
- Website Strategy & Rebuilds
- Conversion UX & Lead Flow
- Brand Identity Systems
- Website Ownership & Optimization

### 3. CRM, Automation & AI
Primary problems:
- leads and systems are disconnected
- processes are manual
- internal tooling is weak
- visibility and follow-up are unreliable

Best child pages:
- CRM Architecture
- Workflow Automation
- Sales Enablement Systems
- Internal Tools & AI Workflows
- Reporting & Decision Systems

### 4. Growth, SEO & Demand
Primary problems:
- the business is not visible enough
- content is not helping growth enough
- conversion is too low
- traffic and lead systems are disconnected

Best child pages:
- Local SEO
- GEO / AI Search Readiness
- Content & Demand Systems
- Conversion Optimization
- Lead Generation Systems

## Layered content rule across homepage → services → sub-service pages

### Homepage
Use mostly Layer 1 + some Layer 2.
Do not lead with Layer 3.

### Services index
Use Layer 1 first, then short Layer 2 translation.
Minimal Layer 3.

### Sub-service pages
Use all three layers in order:
1. buyer language
2. strategic translation
3. technical specificity

## CTA map

| Visitor state | Best route |
|---|---|
| understands the problem, wants proof | `/work` |
| understands the problem, wants the service | `/services` or a child service page |
| unsure and wants help clarifying | `/tools` or `/contact?intent=unsure` |
| has already seen proof | `/contact?intent=work` |
| has already run a tool | `/contact?intent=tool` |

## What should be reduced or avoided

- repeated founder-positioning blocks across homepage sections
- overuse of service labels before the problem is explained
- technical acronyms high on the page without translation
- pages that try to explain every possible engagement at once

## Use case

Use this file when:
