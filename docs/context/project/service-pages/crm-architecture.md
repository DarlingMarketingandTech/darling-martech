# CRM Architecture

## Page role
This page should explain CRM architecture in practical business terms for SMB buyers who know leads are slipping but may not know the structural reason why.

- **Priority:** 1
- **Primary audience:** founder, operator, sales-led service business, growth-stage team with a messy pipeline
- **Primary CTA:** `/contact?intent=service`
- **Primary proof:** `/work/graston-growth-engine`
- **Supporting proof:** `/work/barbershop-command-center`

## H1 direction
CRM architecture that makes lead handling, follow-up, and reporting easier to run.

## Subhead direction
When leads, handoffs, and visibility are scattered across tools or processes, growth gets messy. CRM architecture creates the structure that makes the system usable.

## Layered content
### Layer 1 — plain-English opener
Your CRM should not be the place where leads go to disappear. It should help the team know what is happening, what happens next, and what needs attention.

### Layer 2 — translator section
CRM architecture is the structure behind how leads move, how teams follow up, how data gets trusted, and how sales and marketing stop fighting each other.

### Layer 3 — technical scope
Typical technical scope can include lifecycle stage design, field architecture, pipeline logic, routing rules, automation triggers, source cleanup, integration planning, dashboard views, and reporting structure.

## Why it matters
Poor CRM structure causes lost leads, inconsistent follow-up, weak visibility, and unnecessary manual work. Good structure improves conversion, accountability, and decision quality.

## Signs you need this
- the CRM exists but no one fully trusts it
- pipeline stages are inconsistent or unclear
- leads are being handled differently by different people
- reporting is messy or incomplete
- data is duplicated, missing, or hard to use

## What this usually includes
- current-state CRM audit
- lifecycle and stage mapping
- field and property cleanup
- routing and ownership rules
- automation and handoff review
- dashboard / visibility planning
- documentation and usage guidance
