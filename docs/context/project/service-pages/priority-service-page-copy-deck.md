# Priority Service Page Copy Deck

This file collects tighter, more execution-ready copy guidance for the top 5 priority service pages.

Use it alongside the individual page briefs when drafting or implementing the first wave of child-service pages.

## 1. Fractional CMO / Strategic Leadership

### H1 options
- Fractional CMO leadership for businesses that need senior direction without agency hand-offs.
- Strategic marketing leadership for teams that need a real owner of the whole system.

### Preferred subhead
When marketing activity exists but no one is truly steering the strategy, systems, and priorities underneath it, growth gets harder to trust. I step in as the accountable lead across the whole picture.

### Signs you need this
- the team is busy, but no one owns the whole system
- campaigns happen without a clear strategic sequence
- vendors or freelancers are producing work without enough alignment
- reporting exists, but confidence is low
- leadership keeps changing priorities because the path is not clear

### What this usually includes
- strategic audit and goal alignment
- channel and offer prioritization
- messaging and conversion direction
- reporting and KPI guidance
- vendor / team coordination
- roadmap and executive-level oversight

### FAQ topics
- what does a fractional CMO actually do?
- how is this different from an agency?
- do you implement too or only advise?
- is this right for a small team?

### Proof angle
Use Graston Technique as the main proof of strategic + systems leadership. Use Pike Medical as the supporting proof of long-term growth stewardship.

## 2. Website Strategy & Rebuilds

### H1 options
- Website strategy and rebuilds for businesses that need more trust, clarity, and conversion.
- Websites that do more than look credible — they explain, convert, and support growth.

### Preferred subhead
A rebuild should fix more than visuals. It should clarify the offer, improve trust, support search, and make the next step easier for the right visitor.

### Signs you need this
- the site looks acceptable but underperforms
- pages are generic or hard to understand quickly
- trust is weaker than it should be
- navigation and page flow feel messy
- SEO is weak because the structure is weak

### What this usually includes
- website audit and page strategy
- sitemap and content hierarchy
- messaging and conversion direction
- rebuild/restructure planning
- launch sequencing and QA
- analytics and measurement setup

### FAQ topics
- do I need a full rebuild or a strategic refresh?
- can this work on my current platform?
- do you handle content and structure too?
- how do you measure success after launch?

### Proof angle
Lead with Pike Medical for trust and growth. Support with Riley Bennett Egloff or Tuohy Bailey & Moore for professional-services relevance.

## 3. CRM Architecture

### H1 options
- CRM architecture that makes lead handling, follow-up, and reporting easier to run.
- A cleaner CRM structure for businesses that are tired of dropped leads and messy visibility.

### Preferred subhead
When the CRM is technically there but operationally unreliable, the problem is usually structure. This work fixes how leads move, who owns them, and how the system stays usable.

### Signs you need this
- no one fully trusts the CRM
- pipeline stages are inconsistent or unclear
- follow-up varies too much by person
- reporting is messy or incomplete
- data is duplicated, missing, or hard to use

### What this usually includes
- CRM audit
- stage and lifecycle design
- field/property cleanup
- routing and ownership rules
- automation and handoff review
- visibility and dashboard structure

### FAQ topics
- what is CRM architecture versus CRM setup?
- can this work inside our current CRM?
- do you build the automations too?
- how do I know if this is a CRM issue or a sales-process issue?

### Proof angle
Lead with Graston Growth Engine as the clearest system proof. Support with Barbershop Command Center.

## 4. Local SEO

### H1 options
- Local SEO for businesses that need to be found — and chosen — in the right searches.
- Local visibility that improves trust, not just rankings.

### Preferred subhead
If local search should be producing more business than it is, the problem is usually bigger than keywords. The right fix usually combines page clarity, local trust, and conversion structure.

### Signs you need this
- competitors show up more often in the searches that matter
- service or location pages are too thin
- local visibility is not turning into inquiries
- the business should be better known nearby than it is
- search traffic exists, but action is weak

### What this usually includes
- local SEO audit
- service/location page review
- structure and metadata direction
- internal-linking and FAQ support
- trust and conversion alignment

### FAQ topics
- what does local SEO actually include?
- how is this different from technical SEO?
- do I need dedicated location pages?
- how long does local SEO take to improve?

### Proof angle
Lead with Russell Painting. Support with Hoosier Boy and Pike Medical for local-intent range.

## 5. Conversion Optimization

### H1 options
- Conversion optimization for businesses that are getting attention but not enough action.
- Better paths from visit to inquiry, booking, or purchase.

### Preferred subhead
If the right people are already showing up but too few are taking the next step, the problem is usually friction, structure, or clarity — not a lack of traffic.

### Signs you need this
- traffic is decent, but action is weak
- booking, inquiry, or order paths feel clunky
- too many users drop before the next step
- mobile conversion is weaker than it should be
- key pages have no clear priority path

### What this usually includes
- conversion-path audit
- CTA and action-hierarchy review
- form / booking / order-flow analysis
- page-friction analysis
- UX and action-path recommendations

### FAQ topics
- do I need CRO or a redesign?
- can you improve conversion without rebuilding everything?
- how do you measure conversion gains?
- does this work for lead gen and ecommerce?

### Proof angle
Lead with 317 BBQ. Support with Pike Medical and Hoosier Boy.

## Writing note
Across all top-5 pages:
- H1 should stay plain-English
- subhead should clarify why the problem matters
- signs-you-need-it bullets should be diagnostic, not fluffy
- proof should be framed around service relevance, not just client name
- CTA should usually remain `/contact?intent=service`
