## H1 direction
Fractional CMO leadership for businesses that need senior marketing direction without agency hand-offs.

## Subhead direction
When marketing is active but no one is truly steering the whole system, I step in to connect strategy, execution, websites, CRM, reporting, and growth priorities under one accountable lead.

## Layered content
### Layer 1 — plain-English opener
You do not need more disconnected marketing activity. You need someone senior enough to decide what matters, what happens next, and how the pieces should work together.

### Layer 2 — translator section
This work sits between high-level strategy and day-to-day execution. It means clarifying the offer, setting priorities, directing campaigns and channels, cleaning up the system underneath them, and making performance easier to understand.

### Layer 3 — technical scope
Typical scope can include KPI framework design, channel planning, CRM and automation review, website and conversion review, reporting logic, vendor management, campaign direction, and cross-functional coordination.

## Why it matters
Without senior ownership, marketing tends to become reactive, fragmented, and hard to trust. Fractional leadership prevents wasted spend, disconnected execution, and constant priority drift.

## Signs you need this
- campaigns are happening, but no one owns the full picture
- the team is busy, but progress feels unclear
- agencies or freelancers are producing work without strategic alignment
- leadership wants growth, but priorities change every month
- reporting exists, but no one can confidently explain what is actually working

## What this usually includes
- strategic audit and goal review
- offer and channel prioritization
- messaging and conversion direction
- performance and reporting review
- team / vendor guidance
- roadmap and decision cadence
- ongoing senior oversight or embedded leadership
