Overview
Executive Summary

The research document is essentially a strategic operating manual for where Darling MarTech sits in the market and what it needs to build. It introduces five core service pillars, four distinct client personas, three productized offers, and several named internal frameworks — The Fortress, The Conductor, GEO, agentic workflows — that currently have no direct representation on the website.

The biggest gap between where the site is today (6 generic service categories) and where this research points is specificity. The research gives us the language, the proof, and the positioning to build interior service pages that are genuinely ownable and conversion-ready.

This document translates the research into a concrete parent/child services architecture, with page-level briefs for every proposed URL, built specifically for use as landing pages and targeted social media entry points.

Part 6
Implementation Recommendations for the Dev Build

6.1 Architecture Decisions

  • Extend current routing: Keep /services/[slug] and add /services/[parent]/[child] nested routing
  • Each child page needs its own data entry in data/services.ts — add: parentSlug, hero headline, problem statement, deliverables array, FAQ array, pricing signal, CTA text, relatedServices array
  • Schema is non-negotiable: Every service page gets Service schema; child pages also get FAQ schema
  • Add relatedServices field to each child for cross-linking adjacent services (critical for internal link architecture and GEO)

6.2 Child Page Template Section Order

  1. Hero — H1 + single pain-point statement + primary CTA button
  2. Problem Block — 2–3 sentences defining the problem this service solves
  3. What's Included — Bulleted list of deliverables (scannable, not a wall of text)
  4. Proof Point — One specific stat or outcome from Graston or another client
  5. Process — 3–4 step abbreviated process (reassures buyers)
  6. FAQ — 3 questions, 100–200 word answers each, schema-ready
  7. Pricing Signal — "Investment starts at..." language
  8. CTA Block — Primary CTA + secondary "see related work" link to /work

6.3 GEO-First Writing Standards for Every Page

  • Clear H1/H2/H3 hierarchy with keyword-intentional headings on every page
  • Keep all paragraphs to 2–3 sentences maximum
  • At least one original statistic or benchmarked result per page
  • Link to at least one authoritative external source per page
  • Never block AI crawlers — audit Cloudflare firewall rules
  • Minimum 3 Q&A pairs in FAQ schema per child page

6.4 Social Media Entry Point Strategy

Each child page is a standalone landing page. Treat every URL as a social media destination:

Part 8
Content Differentiation Rules

The research explicitly warns that AI commoditization threatens generic services. Every page must be filtered through these rules.

Every Page Must Avoid
  • Generic benefit lists ("we'll grow your business")
  • Overclaiming without proof ("the best in the industry")
  • Jargon without definition ("synergistic approaches")
  • Talking to "all businesses" without specifying who
Every Page Must Include
  • A named framework or proprietary approach
  • At least one specific metric from real client work
  • Jacob's stated POV on how this service is usually done wrong
  • The specific type of business this page is built for
