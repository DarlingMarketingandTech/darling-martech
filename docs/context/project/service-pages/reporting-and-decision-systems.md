# Reporting & Decision Systems

## Page role
Explain reporting as decision support, not vanity dashboards.

- **Priority:** 3
- **Primary audience:** founder, operator, marketing lead who lacks clear visibility
- **Primary CTA:** `/contact?intent=service`
- **Primary proof:** `/work/graston-technique`
- **Supporting proof:** `/work/the-compass`, `/work/the-conductor` 

## H1 direction
Reporting and decision systems for businesses that need usable visibility, not more disconnected numbers.

## Plain-English definition
This service turns scattered activity and messy reporting into a smaller set of signals that help the business decide what to do next.

## Why it matters
If no one trusts the data, decisions slow down and priorities become political instead of clear.

## Signs you need it
- reporting exists but no one trusts it
- too many dashboards, not enough clarity
- marketing cannot tie work back to outcomes
- leadership cannot quickly answer what is working

## What it usually includes
- KPI and signal design
- reporting review and cleanup
- source alignment
- dashboard direction
- decision cadence support

## Technical scope
Attribution cleanup, source mapping, dashboard architecture, KPI hierarchy, and decision-support reporting.

## Proof block
Use Graston as the primary visibility + systems proof. Support with The Compass / The Conductor as internal decision-system examples if appropriate.
