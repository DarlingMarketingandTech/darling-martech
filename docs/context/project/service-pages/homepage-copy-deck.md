# Homepage Copy Deck

Use this file when rewriting or refining the homepage so it aligns with the new layered content strategy.

## Homepage job
The homepage should:
- orient the right buyer fast
- explain what is broken
- explain what Darling MarTech fixes
- show where proof lives
- offer the right next step

The homepage should **not**:
- repeat the full founder story in multiple sections
- explain every service in detail
- lead with jargon
- try to sell every offer at once

## Recommended homepage sequence
1. Hero
2. Services intro / cluster framing
3. Featured tool
4. Selected proof
5. Testimonials
6. Contact CTA

## 1. Hero

### Section job
Identify the buyer, frame the problem, explain the model, and direct them toward proof or conversation.

### H1 options
1. Strategy, systems, and execution — in one accountable lead.
2. Marketing that actually connects to the systems underneath it.
3. When growth, websites, CRM, and reporting are disconnected, everything gets harder.
4. One person who can fix the strategy and the system.

### Preferred direction
Use a line that combines:
- buyer problem
- model differentiator
- strategic + technical depth

### Subhead options
1. I help small businesses and growing teams fix the gaps between messaging, websites, CRM, automation, and measurement so growth becomes easier to run and easier to trust.
2. Most teams have marketing activity. Fewer have a system that connects positioning, website performance, follow-up, and reporting. That is the gap I fix.
3. For founders and operators who are tired of disconnected tools, weak conversion, and strategy that never quite reaches execution.

### Trust-strip / micro-proof options
Use one quiet supporting line below the hero copy if needed:
- 15+ years building marketing systems that improve conversion, visibility, and operational clarity.
- Strategy, websites, CRM, automation, and measurement — without agency hand-offs.
- +212% qualified leads. 95% overhead reduction. 45% patient growth.

### CTA logic
Primary CTA:
- `See the work`

Secondary CTA:
- `Request a consultation`
- or quiet text link to `/about`

## 2. Services intro / cluster framing

### Section job
Translate the hero promise into buyer-recognizable problem clusters.

### Intro headline options
1. Where the bottlenecks usually are.
2. Four places growth tends to break.
3. The work usually starts in one of these places.

### Intro copy options
1. Most businesses do not need more random marketing activity. They need the right problem solved first — whether that is strategy, website performance, CRM structure, or demand generation.
2. The visible issue is not always the real issue. Weak conversion, unclear messaging, messy systems, and unreliable reporting tend to compound each other.

### Cluster framing rule
Each cluster teaser should start with the business problem first, then the service label.

Example:
- **No senior owner of the whole system** → Strategy & Leadership
- **Website underperforming** → Websites, UX & Brand
- **Leads and follow-up are messy** → CRM, Automation & AI
- **Visibility and demand are too weak** → Growth, SEO & Demand

## 3. Featured tool

### Section job
Give unsure visitors immediate value without sending them straight to contact.

### Recommended framing
- Use the tool as a strategic bridge, not a gimmick.
- Make the utility clear in one sentence.

### Copy directions
- Run the tool if you want to clarify the problem before we talk.
- Start here if you need a clearer diagnosis before choosing a service.
- The fastest way to make the next step easier is to see what the tool surfaces first.

## 4. Selected proof

### Section job
Show a curated, high-signal sample of evidence.

### Headline options
1. Selected proof.
2. A tight edit of the work.
3. The proof behind the offer.

### Support copy options
1. A small set of case studies that show how strategy, systems, and execution connect in practice.
2. These are not every project. They are the clearest proof of what changes when the work is done well.

## 5. Testimonials

### Section job
Add external validation after proof, not before.

### Guidance
- Keep testimonial framing calm.
- Let the client language reinforce strategic depth, working style, and trust.
- Do not make testimonials denser than the proof section.

### Headline options
1. What it feels like to work together.
2. What clients noticed.
3. Why the work sticks.

## 6. Contact CTA

### Section job
Close the loop with one calm invitation.

### CTA headline options
1. If this sounds like the bottleneck, let’s talk.
2. Every serious build starts with clarity.
3. If the problem is clear enough to name, it is probably time to talk.

### Support line options
- No pitch. Just an honest conversation about whether this is a fit.
- I reply within 1 business day, usually faster.

### CTA route
- `/contact?intent=unsure` for broad homepage CTA
- use `/contact?intent=service` only if the CTA is clearly service-led

## Homepage writing rules
- keep founder positioning mostly in the hero
- do not recreate the removed About teaser in another form
- lead with problems and consequences, not category labels
- use one dominant idea per section
- keep secondary sections shorter than the hero/proof combination
