# Darling MarTech Offers And Packaging

This file defines the commercial offer ladder for Darling MarTech.

Its purpose is to help Claude write:
- service pages
- pricing / offer pages
- CTA copy
- contact form categories
- proposals and follow-up copy

This is not a final public pricing page. It is the internal packaging model.

## Offer Strategy

Core rule:
- lead with low-risk, proof-heavy entry offers
- move qualified buyers into foundation or build-out projects
- convert strongest-fit clients into retainers

Pricing rule:
- outcome-framed, never hourly
- use ranges when uncertainty is high
- anchor value to cost of inaction, revenue upside, or time recovered

Primary buyer order:
1. Burned Founder
2. Scaling Operator
3. Tech-Confused CMO
4. Ambitious Newcomer

## 1. Audit Offers

These are the safest entry offers and the easiest pages to sell first.

### MarTech Audit
- Price band:
  - `$2,000–$5,000`
- Who it is for:
  - Burned Founder
  - Tech-Confused CMO
  - Scaling Operator with tool sprawl
- Best fit when:
  - CRM is messy
  - attribution is broken
  - too many tools overlap
  - leads are leaking and nobody knows where
- What is included:
  - stack inventory
  - redundancy analysis
  - lifecycle and handoff review

## 6. Page Selling Rules

- `/services/martech-audit`
  - sells the audit first
  - introduces rationalization build as the natural next step

- `/services/strategy`
  - sells strategic guidance and fractional-style engagements

- `/services/brand-web`
  - sells foundation and rebuild offers

- `/services/systems`
  - sells CRM, automation, and internal-ops build-outs

- `/services/growth`
  - sells search, analytics, and reporting offers

- `/process`
  - explains how audit → build → retainer works

- `/contact`
  - should convert around offer categories, not generic “tell me about your project” copy

- future `/pricing`
  - should present price bands, ideal-fit buyer, and expected outcomes, not rigid fixed-fee promises

---

## 7. What Claude Should Not Do

- Do not write hourly-rate language.
- Do not frame offers like freelancer task lists.
- Do not sell “a website” or “SEO” in isolation when the stronger commercial story is system + outcome.
- Do not default to portfolio-style copy when the page should sell an offer.
- Do not present every service equally; priority remains:
  - MarTech Audit
  - CRM / RevOps build-outs
  - Website conversion rebuilds
  - Analytics / reporting clarity
