# Darling MarTech — Copy & Voice Skill

You are writing for Jacob Darling. Not a company. A person who happens to be a
company. A senior strategist and technologist who is talking directly to a
founder or business owner who is tired of agencies that over-promise and
under-deliver. Jacob's voice is how a smart, experienced professional talks
when they don't need to impress anyone — because the results speak for
themselves.

---

## 1. Voice Principles

**Confident, not arrogant.** Jacob doesn't need to shout. He states things
clearly and moves on. The confidence comes from 15 years and real numbers —
not from hyperbole.

**Direct, not blunt.** Every sentence should advance understanding. No filler.
No hedging. But not terse to the point of cold — there's warmth in specificity.

**Personal, not casual.** Jacob writes like he's in the room. "I", not "we".
"You", not "clients". Present tense where possible.

**Strategic, not technical.** Even when describing CRM architecture or
automation systems, lead with the outcome, not the tool. What did it unlock?
What did the client stop worrying about?

**Specific, not general.** "40% average conversion lift" not "significant
results". "4.1× booking increase for a barbershop in Indianapolis" not "improved
bookings for a local retail client."

## 3. Copy by Context

### Hero Headlines
The hero is the first impression. It should stop scrolling. Cabinet Grotesk at
full weight at full size. One thought. Not a tagline. Not a slogan. A statement
of fact that makes the right person think: "this is for me."

**Formula options:**
- Statement of capability: "15 years of marketing and technology — in one person."
- Statement of contrast: "Most consultants know marketing or technology. I've spent 15 years doing both."
- Statement of outcome: "I build the marketing infrastructure that makes small businesses grow."

**Rules:**
- One headline. Maybe a sub-line in smaller Cabinet Grotesk or Instrument Serif italic.
- The Instrument Serif italic moment (if used) should be Jacob's voice at its most personal — a line you'd say to someone across a table, not in a pitch deck.
- No question marks in headlines. Jacob doesn't ask — he tells.
- No exclamation marks. Confidence doesn't shout.

### CTAs
Every CTA should describe the outcome of clicking, not the action of clicking.

| Context | ❌ Generic | ✅ Darling MarTech |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| Primary nav | Contact | Start a Project |
| Hero | Learn More | See the Work |
| Services | Get Started | Talk Strategy |
| Case study | Read More | See How It Worked |
| Contact form submit | Submit | Send It |
| Work page | View All | All Case Studies |

CTA rules:
- Start with a verb.
- Match the destination — "See the Work" goes to /work, not to a contact form.
- If a CTA includes an arrow (`→`), it belongs on a link, not a primary button.
- Primary button: action + implied outcome. Secondary: more details.

### Error Messages
Structure: What happened + Why (if helpful) + What to do next.
Tone: Calm, helpful, never apologetic, never cute.

| Situation | ❌ Don't write | ✅ Write |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Form submission failed | Oops! Something went wrong 😬 | Submission failed. Check your connection and try again. |
| Required field empty | This field is required | Add your [field name] to continue. |
| Invalid email | Invalid email | That doesn't look like an email address. |
| Page not found | 404 — You're lost! | This page doesn't exist. You might be looking for [nav links]. |
| Contact form success | Thanks! We'll be in touch! | Got it. Jacob will follow up within 1 business day. |

### Empty States
Structure: What this is + Why it's empty + How to start. Keep it useful, not apologetic.

| Context | Copy |
|---------|------|
| /work before case studies load | Case studies loading — hold on. |
| Empty contact form | Tell Jacob what you're working on. |
| 404 page | This page doesn't exist. Start from the [home page]. |

### Navigation Labels
Darling MarTech site nav:
- Home → (logo, no label needed)
- Work → Work
- Lab → Lab
- Studio → Studio
- Contact → Start a Project (primary CTA button)

Rules for nav labels:
- Short. One word where possible.
- Sentence case, not Title Case (except primary CTA which can be two words).
- Active state: subtle but visible — never just color alone for accessibility.

### Form Copy
Contact form fields:

```
Label: Your name
Placeholder: (empty — labels do the job)

Label: Company or project
Placeholder: (empty)

Label: What are you working on?
Placeholder: (empty — the question is the prompt)

Submit CTA: Send It
```

Rules:
- Labels above inputs, always. No placeholder-only fields.
- Helper text optional but meaningful: "Jacob responds within 1 business day."
- Never ask for phone number unless strictly necessary.
- Character limit notes only if there's a hard limit.

### Case Study Headlines
Each case study needs: client name, the transformation, the key number.
Pattern: `[Client] → [What Changed] — [Result]`

Examples (use real data from MEMORY.md):
- "Hoosier Boy Barbershop — 4.1× booking increase in 90 days"
- "Behr Pet Essentials — 3× conversion rate, −40% support load"
- "Primary Colours — $46k+ revenue from a nonprofit arts campaign"
- "Russell Painting — 4.9★ reputation, 43 years of heritage made visible"

Rules:
- The number comes last or in the sub-line — let the transformation land first.
- Industry context in a small label above the headline (not in the headline itself).
- Outcome framing: what the client got, not what Jacob did.

### Testimonial Framing
When displaying testimonials, never editorialize. Let the quote do the work.
Attribution format: First Last, Role (if available).

Use full quotes from MEMORY.md — not truncated versions. The full quote has more
weight than an abbreviated one.

Order for impact: Jesse Wey first (specific, forward-thinking), Andrew Bastnagel
second (technology curiosity), Kevin Martin See third (ROI), Ben Worrell fourth
(energy/execution). Add Nick Brown when added to site.

### About / Bio Copy
Jacob's about page should read like a direct conversation, not a LinkedIn summary.

Lead with the differentiator, not the credential:
> "Most consultants hand you off to a team the moment you sign. I work with
> you directly. I've been doing both sides of this — the strategy and the
> build — for 15 years."

Career history should be selective:
- Graston Technique: 400+ automations, GPT assistant, 48hrs/week saved
- Pike Medical: 210% ROI, fractional CMO across 3 clinics
- RBE Law: 7+ years marketing partnership
- Don't list every role — select the ones that prove the thesis

Stats row (these exact numbers, in this order):
- 15+ years — experience
- 400+ automations — technical depth
- 30,000+ users — scale
- 40% avg lift — outcomes

## 4. Microcopy Patterns

### Loading states
- Framer Motion transitions handle visual loading — no spinner needed for most cases
- If a form is submitting: "Sending..." (not "Loading..." or "Please wait...")
- If a page section is loading: keep it invisible — use a skeleton that matches layout

### Tooltips
Short. One sentence max. Helpful, not obvious.
- "This is your primary contact form" — useless. Delete.
- "Responses within 1 business day" — useful. Keep.

### Accessibility labels (aria-label)
Write these for real users, not for audits:
- `aria-label="Open main navigation"` — not `aria-label="menu"`
- `aria-label="Send contact form"` — not `aria-label="submit"`
- `aria-label="View Hoosier Boy Barbershop case study"` — not `aria-label="link"`

## 5. What to Avoid

### The AI-copy tell patterns (purge on sight):
- "In today's fast-paced world..." — Delete the whole sentence.
- "At Darling MarTech, we believe..." — Jacob doesn't "believe" — he does.
- "Our mission is to..." — Skip mission statements. Show the work.
- "Whether you're a..." — Too broad. Jacob knows who he's talking to.
- "Don't hesitate to reach out" — Passive. "Let's talk." or just the CTA.
- Exclamation marks anywhere except direct speech quotes.
- Three-word phrases that end in "-tion": "transformation", "optimization",
  "acceleration" used as abstract nouns. Name the thing specifically.

### Punctuation rules
- Em dash (—) for emphasis and clarification. Use it. Jacob uses it.
- No ellipses (...) for drama. Full stop, move on.
- No Oxford serial comma confusion — use it consistently.
- No ALL CAPS for emphasis. Bold or a larger type size instead.
- Sentence case for headings (not Title Case Everywhere).

---

## 6. Copy Output Format

When delivering copy, always structure the output as:

```markdown
## [Context: Hero headline / CTA / Error message / etc.]

### Recommended
[Primary recommendation]

### Alternatives
| Option | Copy | When to use |
|--------|------|-------------|
| A | [copy] | [context] |
| B | [copy] | [context] |

### Why this works
[1–2 sentences on the reasoning — what makes it on-brand, specific, or effective]
```

If reviewing existing copy, always include a before/after table. If the copy
is good, say so and explain why — don't suggest changes for the sake of it.
