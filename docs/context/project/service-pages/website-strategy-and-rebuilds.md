## H1 direction
Website strategy and rebuilds for businesses that need more trust, clarity, and conversion — not just a nicer design.

## Subhead direction
A high-performing website should explain the offer clearly, support search visibility, and move the right visitor toward action.

## Layered content
### Layer 1 — plain-English opener
If the site looks acceptable but still fails to build trust or convert visitors, the problem is usually structural, not just visual.

### Layer 2 — translator section
A rebuild is not only a design project. It is message clarity, page architecture, conversion flow, search visibility, and operational fit.

### Layer 3 — technical scope
Typical technical scope can include sitemap design, service-page architecture, CMS planning, lead-flow mapping, SEO structure, analytics setup, conversion-path planning, and content hierarchy.

## Why it matters
The website is usually the place where trust gets made or lost. A weak site lowers conversion, makes SEO harder, and forces sales or intake teams to compensate for structural problems.

## Signs you need this
- the site looks fine but does not produce enough inquiries
- pages feel generic or hard to understand
- navigation and page flow are messy
- SEO is weak because the structure is weak
- the site is difficult to update or scale

## What this usually includes
- site audit and goal review
- sitemap and page strategy
- messaging direction
- conversion-path planning
- design / rebuild direction
- launch sequencing and QA
- analytics and measurement setup
