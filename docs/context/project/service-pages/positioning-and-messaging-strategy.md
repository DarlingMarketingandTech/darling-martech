# Positioning & Messaging Strategy

## Page role
Explain this service as business clarity work, not fluffy brand language.

- **Priority:** 2
- **Primary audience:** founder, owner-led business, repositioning service brand
- **Primary CTA:** `/contact?intent=service`
- **Primary proof:** `/work/hoosier-boy-barbershop`
- **Supporting proof:** `/work/black-letter`, `/work/clean-aesthetic`

## H1 direction
Positioning and messaging strategy for businesses that need to be understood faster and chosen more often.

## Plain-English definition
This service clarifies what the business says, who it is for, and why the right buyer should care.

## Why it matters
Weak positioning makes every page, campaign, and sales conversation harder than it should be.

## Signs you need it
- the site sounds generic
- the offer is hard to explain simply
- the wrong leads keep showing up
- the business looks credible but not distinctive

## What it usually includes
- offer clarification
- audience and category framing
- message hierarchy
- core claims and differentiators
- service-page and homepage direction

## Technical scope
Message architecture, value proposition structure, conversion copy mapping, and page-level content systems.

## Proof block
Use Hoosier Boy for positioning tied to bookings and local differentiation. Use Black Letter and Clean Aesthetic as supporting identity/messaging proof.
