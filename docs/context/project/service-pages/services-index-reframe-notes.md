# Services Index Reframe Notes

Use this file when rewriting or restructuring the main `/services` page so it aligns with the new layered service-page system.

## Goal

The services index should not read like an internal capability list.
It should help the right buyer recognize their problem cluster quickly, understand what kind of help Darling MarTech provides, and move into the correct child page.

## Core shift

### Old tendency
- list service categories first
- explain the business after that
- rely on internal labels too early

### Better direction
- problem first
- service cluster second
- child page options third
- proof and CTA fourth

## Recommended sequence

1. **Intro framing**
   - explain what kinds of problems Darling MarTech solves
   - clarify the strategy + systems + execution model in plain language
2. **Service clusters**
   - Strategy & Leadership
   - Websites, UX & Brand
   - CRM, Automation & AI
   - Growth, SEO & Demand
3. **Child pages**
   - route users into the right sub-service page
4. **Proof support**
   - one short proof anchor or related case per cluster
5. **CTA**
   - `/contact?intent=service`

## Cluster-level framing guidance

### Strategy & Leadership
Use for visitors who feel:
- there is no senior owner of the whole system
- priorities are unclear
- execution exists, but direction is weak

### Websites, UX & Brand
Use for visitors who feel:
- the site is underperforming
- trust is weak
- the offer is not clear enough
- conversion is lower than it should be

### CRM, Automation & AI
Use for visitors who feel:
- systems do not connect
- leads and handoffs are messy
- manual work is slowing the business down
- internal process complexity is too high

### Growth, SEO & Demand
Use for visitors who feel:
- visibility is weak
- content is not doing enough
- conversion needs help
- demand and systems are not working together

## Language rules

### Use more of this
- practical buyer problems
- plain explanations
- specific outcomes
- translator language that connects business and systems

### Use less of this
- jargon high on the page
- category names without explanation
- long lists of deliverables before the reader understands why they matter
- service descriptions that sound like generic agency capability statements

## Recommended CTA logic

### Primary
- `/contact?intent=service`

### Supporting
- one proof link per cluster where useful
- sibling child-page links for visitors who need more specificity
