# Local SEO

## Page role
This page should explain local SEO as a visibility + conversion service for service businesses, not as abstract ranking work.

- **Priority:** 1
- **Primary audience:** local service businesses, clinics, regional firms, local retail brands
- **Primary CTA:** `/contact?intent=service`
- **Primary proof:** `/work/russell-painting`
- **Supporting proof:** `/work/hoosier-boy-barbershop`, `/work/pike-medical-consultants`

## H1 direction
Local SEO for businesses that need to be found — and chosen — in the right searches.

## Subhead direction
Ranking is only part of the job. Local SEO should also improve trust, page clarity, and the number of people who actually call, book, or inquire.

## Layered content
### Layer 1 — plain-English opener
If local search should be producing business and it is not, the problem is usually bigger than keywords. It is often page clarity, local trust, structure, and conversion.

### Layer 2 — translator section
This work connects local search visibility with service-page structure, page usefulness, and action paths so that searchers become leads instead of just impressions.

### Layer 3 — technical scope
Typical technical scope can include local page architecture, metadata direction, location/service structure, internal linking, FAQ layering, content clarity, and conversion alignment.

## Why it matters
Local SEO is one of the clearest ways for the right business to capture existing demand. But visibility without trust and conversion still underperforms.

## Signs you need this
- you should be showing up locally better than you are
- service pages are thin or unclear
- competitors outrank you for important local terms
- traffic exists but inquiry volume is weak
- Google Business visibility is not translating into action

## What this usually includes
- local SEO audit
- service and location page review
- page architecture recommendations
- metadata / internal-linking cleanup
- FAQ and search-intent support
- trust and conversion alignment
